# Scrum Master — Common Challenges & Solutions

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: "Nobody talks in my retrospectives"

**Situation:** Team gives one-word answers. "Everything's fine." Long silences. SM asks "What could be better?" — nobody speaks.

**Root cause:** Team doesn't feel psychologically safe. Retrospectives feel evaluative, not collaborative.

**Solution:**

**Immediate:**
- Switch to **anonymous formats** (online sticky notes via Miro or a Teams poll): people speak more honestly when not identified
- **Set safety explicitly:** "What's said in this retro is between us. The goal is to improve how we work — not to evaluate anyone."
- **Start positive:** ask "What went well?" first to warm the room before challenges
- **Use specific prompts** instead of open questions: "Name one thing that slowed you down this sprint" beats "What could be better?"

**Long-term:**
- Consistency builds safety — if retrospective actions are followed up, team starts trusting the process
- Share retro outcomes with the team to show that feedback leads to change
- Rotate facilitation methods: different formats keep retrospectives fresh

### Challenge 2: "The team ignores the Daily Standup"

**Situation:** People show up late, give long status reports, check their phones, go off on technical tangents. The standup takes 40 minutes and nobody feels it adds value.

**Root cause:** Standup has become a status report TO the SM, rather than a team synchronization FOR the team.

**Solution:**

**Immediate:**
- **Visible timer** on screen: keeps everyone honest about 15 minutes
- **Walk the board** instead of going round-the-room: "What's blocking this story from moving to Done?" is more engaging than "What did you do yesterday?"
- **Start on time, every time** — even if 2 people are present. Lateness becomes a habit when it's tolerated.
- **Parking lot for technical discussions:** "That's important — let's talk after standup. Who needs to be in that conversation?"

**Long-term:**
- Address persistent disengagement in Retrospective: "How do we make standup worth attending?"
- Sometimes the problem is simpler: wrong time of day, poor video setup, or the format just doesn't fit the team

### Challenge 3: "The PO isn't preparing stories in time"

**Situation:** Sprint Planning arrives. Top 10 stories don't meet Definition of Ready. Sprint Planning turns into a 3-hour requirements session. Team starts Sprint without clarity.

**Root cause:** PO isn't allocating time to backlog refinement, or doesn't know what "ready" looks like.

**Solution:**

**Immediate:**
- **Pre-Planning check** (2 days before): "PO, let's do a quick 15-minute review — are these stories meeting our Definition of Ready?" This catches problems before Planning.
- **Make the cost visible:** "Last sprint, we spent 90 minutes in Planning on clarification. If stories were Ready, we'd have used that time for technical discussion."
- If stories aren't ready at Planning time: "We can't commit to this story — the acceptance criteria aren't clear enough. Can we put it back in refinement?"

**Long-term:**
- **Protect refinement sessions:** don't cancel them when the sprint is busy. "1 hour of refinement saves 2 hours of Planning."
- **Retrospective:** if it happens twice, raise it as a process issue: "Our stories keep arriving at Planning without meeting our DoR. What can we change?"

### Challenge 4: "Two team members are in conflict"

**Situation:** Two team members disagree repeatedly, there's visible tension in standups, and it's affecting the team's atmosphere.

**Root cause:** Could be anything — different work styles, unclear responsibilities, accumulated frustration.

**Solution:**

**Never ignore it** — interpersonal tension that isn't addressed grows.

**Step 1:** Private 1:1 with each person separately:
> "I've noticed some tension between you and [name]. Can you help me understand your perspective? I'm not here to judge — I want to understand."

**Step 2:** If both are open to it, facilitate a structured conversation:
> "I want to help you both work better together. Can we talk through this together for 20 minutes? The goal is to understand each other, not to win."

**Step 3:** Identify the root: is it a process problem (unclear responsibilities) or a personal one?
- Process problem → fix the process (clarify the DoD, update a workflow)
- Personal conflict → focus on professional behavior: "We don't have to like each other, but we need to be able to collaborate effectively"

**Step 4:** If it escalates or you're out of your depth: **involve staff immediately**. This is exactly what your staff coach is there for.
